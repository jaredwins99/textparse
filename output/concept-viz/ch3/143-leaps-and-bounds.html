Book Quote
"An efficient algorithm—the leaps and bounds procedure—makes this feasible for p as large as 30 or 40." — ESL §3.3.1, Hastie, Tibshirani & Friedman

Best-subset selection with p predictors nominally requires searching \(2^p\) subsets. For \(p=40\), that is over 1 trillion subsets. Leaps and bounds makes this tractable by maintaining a global best-RSS bound and pruning entire subtrees that cannot beat it.

Guarantees
✓
Globally Optimal
Guaranteed to find the best-RSS subset of each size — unlike greedy stepwise methods.
~
Unbiased (subset-conditional)
OLS on the chosen subset is unbiased conditional on the subset, but selection induces bias.
✗
Low Variance
Discrete selection causes high variance — small data changes can flip the chosen subset entirely.
~
Scalable beyond p = 40
Practical limit ~30–40. For larger p, use LASSO or forward stepwise.

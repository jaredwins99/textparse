Soft Thresholding

ID 151 Section 3.1 estimation / optimization Lasso Solution Operator
\( \hat{\beta}_j = \text{sign}(\hat{\beta}_j^{\text{OLS}})\bigl(|\hat{\beta}_j^{\text{OLS}}| - \lambda\bigr)_+ \)    where \((x)_+ = \max(x, 0)\)

Method Comparison

MethodOperatorZeros?Bias?Corresponds to
Soft (Lasso) \( \text{sign}(z)(|z|-\lambda)_+ \) Yes — exact zeros Yes — shrinks all L1 penalty
Hard (Best Subset) \( z \cdot \mathbf{1}[|z| > \lambda] \) Yes — exact zeros No — keeps full value L0 penalty
Ridge (Shrinkage) \( z / (1 + \lambda) \) No — never exactly zero Yes — shrinks all L2 penalty

Book Quote

"Lasso translates each coefficient by a constant factor λ, truncating at zero. This is called 'soft thresholding'." — ESL, Section 3.1

Guarantees

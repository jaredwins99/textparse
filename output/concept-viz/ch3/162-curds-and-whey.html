Curds and Whey

ID 162  ·  ESL Section 3.7 (Breiman & Friedman 1997)  ·  Category: comparison / aggregation

Prediction surface: Hard truncation (RRR) vs Smooth shrinkage (Curds & Whey)

Hard truncation at rank M sets coefficients to 0 or 1 abruptly. Curds & whey smoothly interpolates — keeping high-correlation canonical directions close to 1 and shrinking low-correlation ones toward 0.

Relative MSE: OLS=1. Lower is better. Vary sample size n/p and true rank.

Curds & whey gains most when n/p is small (high estimation variance). At large n/p, all methods approach OLS. RRR is optimal when true rank is exact; C&W is more robust.

Book Quote

"Curds and whey involves shrinkage of the canonical variates between X and Y, a smooth version of reduced rank regression."

— ESL §3.7 (Breiman & Friedman 1997)

Shrinkage Spectrum

ρ=0 (s→0) ρ=0.5 ρ=1 (s→1)

High canonical correlation → keep direction (s≈1). Low canonical correlation → shrink heavily (s≈0). Unlike RRR there is no hard cutoff.

Key Facts

  • Named after Breiman & Friedman (1997)
  • Shrinkage factor depends on ρₖ and p/n ratio
  • sₖ → 1 as ρₖ → 1 (keep strong directions)
  • sₖ → 0 as ρₖ → 0 or n/p → small
  • Special case of a James-Stein-type estimator
  • More robust than RRR when rank is unknown

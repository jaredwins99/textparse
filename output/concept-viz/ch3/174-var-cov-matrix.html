Variance-Covariance Matrix of Least Squares Estimates

\( \text{Var}(\hat{\boldsymbol\beta}) = (X^\top X)^{-1}\sigma^2 \)

The ellipse shows the 95% confidence region for (β̂₁, β̂₂). As |ρ| → 1, the matrix (XᵀX)⁻¹ blows up, elongating the ellipse — coefficient estimates become wildly uncertain even though the fit is fine. VIF = 1/(1−ρ²) is the variance inflation factor.
Book Quotes
"The variance–covariance matrix of the least squares parameter estimates is Var(β̂) = (XᵀX)⁻¹σ²" — ESL §3.1
"We estimate σ² by σ̂² = Σᵢ(yᵢ − ŷᵢ)²/(N−p−1). The N−p−1 rather than N−p in the denominator makes σ̂² an unbiased estimate." — ESL §3.1
Statistical Properties
YES BLUE — Gauss-Markov theorem
YES Unbiased: E[β̂] = β
YES Consistent (N → ∞)
COND Low variance only if XᵀX well-conditioned
CAUTION Variance explodes under multicollinearity
BLUE = Best Linear Unbiased Estimator. Even with large variance, OLS is still unbiased — but useless for inference when predictors are nearly collinear.

Bias² increases as model is more regularized/restricted (underfitting). Variance increases as model is less regularized (overfitting). Total MSE = Bias² + Variance + σ² (irreducible noise). The dashed vertical line shows your current λ/k setting. Drag the slider to find the minimum.
Book Quotes
"Prediction accuracy can sometimes be improved by shrinking or setting some coefficients to zero. By sacrificing a little bias we can sometimes get a substantial reduction in variance." — ESL §3.1
"Ordinary least squares is unbiased, but often has high variance. We can trade some bias for variance to improve overall prediction accuracy." — ESL §3.1 (paraphrase)
Statistical Properties
YES OLS: unbiased but possibly high variance
TRADEOFF Ridge: biased, lower variance
TRADEOFF Lasso: biased, sparse, variable selection
KEY Optimal λ minimizes EPE (not bias alone)
NO No free lunch — must pick one to hurt other
MSE(θ̃) = Var(θ̃) + Bias²(θ̃). The irreducible noise σ² sets a floor. No estimator can be unbiased AND minimum variance simultaneously (unless the estimator is UMVUE).

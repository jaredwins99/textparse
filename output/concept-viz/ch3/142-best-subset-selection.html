Key insight: The best subset of size k is not necessarily a subset of the best of size k+1. This non-monotone property distinguishes best-subset from greedy methods like forward stepwise.

Key Facts

Definition

"Best subset regression finds for each k the subset of size k that gives smallest residual sum of squares."

Complexity

Requires evaluating all 2p subsets. For p=40 this is ~1012 subsets — infeasible by brute force, requiring algorithmic tricks (see Leaps & Bounds).

Discrete nature

The selection indicator is binary — a variable is either in or out. No shrinkage, no partial inclusion.

Guarantees & Properties

Unbiased (within subset)
OLS on the selected subset is unbiased for the true coefficients of included variables.
Model-selection consistent
Selects the correct model with probability → 1 as n → ∞ (given correct model in candidate set).
Low variance
Lower than full model when truth is sparse, but discrete jumps can produce high variance in variable selection.
Prediction consistent
Not optimal for prediction. Ridge/Lasso often dominate in prediction MSE due to continuous shrinkage.

Related Concepts

  • prerequisite Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) — best subset uses OLS on each candidate subset
  • alternative Leaps and Bounds — efficient algorithm enabling best-subset for p up to 30–40
  • alternative Forward Stepwise Selection — greedy approximation, computationally cheaper
  • alternative Ridge Regression — continuous shrinkage, often better prediction
  • alternative Lasso — continuous + sparse, often preferred over best subset
  • uses AIC Criterion — used to choose optimal k

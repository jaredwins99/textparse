Dantzig Selector

What this fixes vs. vanilla Lasso

Benefits

Designed for the p >> n regime. The constraint bounds the max correlation of any predictor with the residual, which scales better than L2 residual loss when you have far more predictors than observations.
Near-oracle prediction risk under RIP (Restricted Isometry Property). Candes and Tao (2007) show that in compressed sensing settings, the Dantzig selector achieves near-optimal estimation.
Solved as a linear program -- no custom solvers needed, just off-the-shelf LP.

Tradeoffs

Coefficient paths are non-monotone and erratic. Unlike Lasso's clean piecewise-linear paths via LARS, Dantzig paths can oscillate as the slack parameter changes.
RIP is nearly impossible to verify in practice for real design matrices. The theoretical guarantees are strong but hard to check.
Same L1 shrinkage bias as Lasso -- large true coefficients are still penalized toward zero.
"Candes and Tao (2007) proposed the Dantzig selector... They show that it has attractive theoretical properties... [but] coefficient profiles are not monotone, and may be erratic." -- ESL, Section 3.8.5

Constraint geometry: Dantzig vs. Lasso

Lasso minimizes residual sum of squares subject to an L1 ball constraint on coefficients. The Dantzig selector flips the roles: minimize L1 norm of coefficients subject to no predictor being too correlated with the residual. The L1 ball (blue diamond) is the Lasso constraint. The dashed yellow box is the Dantzig feasible region: all coefficient vectors where no single predictor has correlation > s with the residual. The Dantzig solution is the smallest L1 ball touching this box. As s shrinks, the box tightens and both solutions converge; as s grows, the Dantzig solution can differ substantially from Lasso.

Formula reference

\( \min_{\beta}\; \|\beta\|_1 \quad \text{subject to} \quad \|X^\top(y - X\beta)\|_\infty \le s \)

Compare to Lasso: \(\min_\beta \frac{1}{2}\|y - X\beta\|_2^2 + \lambda\|\beta\|_1\). Dantzig minimizes L1 norm (sparsity) subject to an L-infinity constraint (max gradient). Lasso minimizes squared error subject to (or penalized by) L1 norm.

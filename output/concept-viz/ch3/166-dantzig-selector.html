Dantzig Selector

The ℓ∞ constraint

The constraint ‖Xᵀr‖∞ ≤ s (where r = y − Xβ) says each predictor's inner product with the residual is bounded by s. Equivalently, no predictor is too correlated with the residual. The L₁ ball (rotated square) shows where the objective lives. The solution is the smallest L₁ ball touching the feasible region.

5 0.7

Erratic coefficient paths

Unlike Lasso (which has piecewise-linear, monotone paths), Dantzig selector paths can be non-monotone: a coefficient that entered the model may shrink or change sign as s increases. ESL notes this erratic behavior as a key difference. Each colored line is one coefficient β̂ⱼ(s).

Book Quote

"Candès and Tao (2007) proposed a criterion similar to the LAR/lasso, calling the solution the Dantzig selector. They show that it has attractive theoretical properties for high-dimensional problems." — ESL, Section 3.1 / 3.8.1
"The Dantzig selector has some undesirable properties: for example, as the bound s is increased, the coefficient profiles are not monotone, and may be erratic." — ESL, Chapter 3
"The constraint can be viewed as a bound on the inner product of each predictor with the residuals — a form of 'no predictor is too correlated with the residuals.'" — ESL, Chapter 3

Guarantees

~
Near-Oracle Risk (RIP)
Candes-Tao show near-optimal prediction risk under the Restricted Isometry Property — but RIP is hard to verify in practice.
✓
Works for p > n
Designed for high-dimensional settings where p ≫ n. The L∞ constraint scales better than L₂ residuals in this regime.
✓
Sparsity (L₁ objective)
Minimizing ‖β‖₁ promotes sparse solutions — most coefficients are driven to zero, like Lasso.
✗
Not Unbiased
The L₁ penalty introduces shrinkage bias, similar to Lasso. No guarantee of unbiasedness for selected variables.
✗
Erratic Paths
Coefficient paths as a function of s are non-monotone. Unlike Lasso (LARS), there is no clean piecewise-linear solution path.
~
Consistent (conditions)
Variable selection consistency requires sparsity conditions on the true β and incoherence conditions on X, similar to Lasso.

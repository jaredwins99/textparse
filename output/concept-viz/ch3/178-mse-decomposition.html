Mean Squared Error Decomposition

\( \text{MSE}(\tilde{\theta}) = \text{Var}(\tilde{\theta}) + [\mathbb{E}(\tilde{\theta}) - \theta]^2 \)

Each dot = one estimate from a different sample. Bias = systematic off-center error = [E(θ̃) − θ]². Variance = spread of estimates around their mean. Both contribute to MSE. An unbiased estimator (OLS) has dots centered but possibly spread. A biased estimator (ridge) shifts the center but can cluster tighter.

Book Quotes
"MSE(θ̃) = E(θ̃ − θ)² = [E(θ̃) − θ]² + Var(θ̃). The first term is the squared bias, while the second term is the variance." — ESL §3.1
"It is not hard to show that a more complex model will decrease bias but increase variance. There is often a sweet spot in the middle." — ESL §3.1 (paraphrase)
Statistical Properties
FACT MSE always ≥ 0
FACT Unbiased ⟺ Bias² = 0
NOTE Unbiased ≠ minimum MSE
KEY Shrinkage can reduce MSE despite bias
CAUTION Cannot simultaneously minimize both
James-Stein estimator: beats OLS in MSE for p ≥ 3 dimensions by introducing bias. This was a surprising result that motivated modern regularization theory.

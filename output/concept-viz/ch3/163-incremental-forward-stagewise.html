Incremental Forward Stagewise Regression

ESL §3.8 Slow greedy fitting with step-size ε Forward stagewise / FSε
\(\hat\beta_j \leftarrow \hat\beta_j + \varepsilon \cdot \mathrm{sign}\bigl(\langle x_j, r \rangle\bigr), \quad j^* = \arg\max_j |\langle x_j, r \rangle|\)

Large ε = few aggressive steps, rough paths. Small ε = many smooth micro-steps, paths converge to the lasso (ε → 0 gives FS₀ = infinitesimal stagewise).

Adjust ε to see how step size controls the trade-off: large ε fits quickly but overshoots; small ε produces smooth monotone paths closely tracking the lasso solution. At ε → 0 this becomes FS₀.

Book Quote

"repeatedly updating (by a small amount ε) the coefficient of the variable most correlated with the current residuals" — ESL §3.8, p. 86

FSε is slow and deliberate: it never adjusts a coefficient by more than ε at one time. This conservative updating is what makes paths smooth and regularization-like.

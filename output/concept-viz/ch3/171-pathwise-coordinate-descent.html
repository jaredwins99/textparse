Pathwise Coordinate Descent

ESL §3.8 Friedman, Hastie, Höfling & Tibshirani 2007 Fast lasso solver via warm starts
\(\tilde\beta_j(\lambda) \leftarrow S\!\left(\sum_i x_{ij}r_{ij},\; \lambda\right)\!, \quad r_{ij} = y_i - \sum_{k\ne j} x_{ik}\hat\beta_k\)

Pathwise coordinate descent solves lasso for a decreasing sequence of λ values. At each λ, warm-start with the solution from the previous (larger) λ — nearby solutions need only a few CD sweeps to converge. This makes the full path computation much faster than solving each λ from scratch.

Book Quote

"fix the penalty parameter λ and optimize successively over each parameter, holding the other parameters fixed at their current values" — ESL §3.8, p. 89

The soft-thresholding update is the exact closed-form solution for each coordinate. With standardized predictors, one full sweep over all p parameters is O(np). Convergence is guaranteed by coordinate-wise sufficiency.

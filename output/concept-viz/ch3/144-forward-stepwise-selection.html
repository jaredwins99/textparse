ID 144 § 3.3.2 — Subset Selection Comparison / Selection

Forward Stepwise Selection

At each step: add \(\hat{j} = \arg\min_{j \notin S} \text{RSS}(S \cup \{j\})\)  |  Start from intercept, greedily grow the model
Step-by-Step: Variables Added & RSS Decreasing
Press Play to watch variables enter the model one at a time, and see RSS drop at each step.
Book Quote
"Forward-stepwise selection starts with the intercept, and then sequentially adds into the model the predictor that most improves the fit." — ESL §3.3.2, Hastie, Tibshirani & Friedman

Computationally cheaper than best-subset: only \(1 + p + (p-1) + \cdots = O(p^2)\) models fit, vs \(2^p\). Greedy — may miss the globally optimal subset, but in practice performs comparably.

Guarantees
✗
Globally Optimal
Not guaranteed — greedy. May miss the best subset for a given size.
~
Unbiased
OLS within chosen subset is unbiased conditional on selection, but selection itself is adaptive.
✗
Low Variance
Discrete nature causes high variance — small data perturbations can change which variables enter.
✓
Monotone Fit Improvement
Training RSS cannot increase — each added variable can only help (or not change) the fit.
✓
Scales to Large p
Works for p > 40 where best-subset and leaps-and-bounds are infeasible.

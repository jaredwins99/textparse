Canonical Correlation Analysis

\(\max_{a,b} \; \mathrm{Corr}(\mathbf{X}a,\, \mathbf{Y}b)\)  s.t.  \(\mathrm{Var}(\mathbf{X}a)=\mathrm{Var}(\mathbf{Y}b)=1\)   —   \(\rho_k = \sigma_k(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_{xx}^{-1/2}\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_{xy}\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_{yy}^{-1/2})\)
Canonical Variates
Canonical Correlations

X-space and Y-space canonical directions (2D projection)

Each canonical pair (Uₖ, Vₖ) maximizes correlation between the X-linear combination Uₖ=Xaₖ and Y-linear combination Vₖ=Ybₖ, subject to orthogonality to previous pairs.

Canonical correlations ρ₁ ≥ ρ₂ ≥ … ≥ ρₖ (decreasing)

Canonical correlations are the singular values of \(\Sigma_{xx}^{-1/2}\Sigma_{xy}\Sigma_{yy}^{-1/2}\). They are always ordered and at most min(p,q) of them.

Book Quote

"CCA finds uncorrelated linear combinations with successively maximized correlations. The solution is obtained via a generalized SVD of the cross-covariance matrix."

— ESL §3.7

Key Facts

  • K = min(p, q) canonical pairs at most
  • ρ₁ ≥ ρ₂ ≥ … ≥ ρ_K ≥ 0 always
  • Generalizes simple correlation to multivariate
  • Solved via SVD of whitened cross-covariance
  • CCA with K directions used in reduced-rank regression
  • Sensitive to small sample size (n must be large)

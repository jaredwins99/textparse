Z-Score for Regression Coefficients

\( z_j = \dfrac{\hat{\beta}_j}{\sqrt{v_j \hat{\sigma}^2}} \)
where \( v_j = [(X^\top X)^{-1}]_{jj} \) and \( \hat{\sigma}^2 = \frac{1}{N-p-1}\sum_i(y_i - \hat{y}_i)^2 \)
Z-Scores by Predictor — Significance at |z| > 2
Dashed lines at z = ±2 approximate the 5% significance threshold. Under H₀: βⱼ = 0, z_j ~ t_{N-p-1}. Colors: ■ significant · ■ not significant.
Book Quotes
"To test the hypothesis that a particular coefficient β_j = 0, we form the standardized coefficient or Z-score" — ESL §3.2, Hastie, Tibshirani, Friedman
"z_j is distributed as t_{N−p−1} under the null hypothesis that β_j = 0. A large (absolute) value of z_j will lead us to reject this null hypothesis." — ESL §3.2
Statistical Properties
YES t-distributed under normality
COND Valid under Gauss-Markov + normality
NO Not robust to heavy tails
COND Consistent (N → ∞)
CAUTION Multiple testing inflates false positives
Under H₀: βⱼ = 0 with Gaussian errors, z_j ~ t_{N−p−1}. For large N, t ≈ z (standard normal). The |z| > 2 rule corresponds roughly to α = 0.05 two-sided.

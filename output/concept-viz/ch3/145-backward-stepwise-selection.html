ID 145 § 3.3.2 — Subset Selection Comparison / Selection

Backward Stepwise Selection

At each step: remove \(\hat{j} = \arg\min_{j \in S}\, |z_j|\) where \(z_j = \hat{\beta}_j / \hat{\sigma}\sqrt{v_j}\)  |  Start full, shrink greedily
Z-scores per Variable — Remove Lowest at Each Step
Press Play. At each step the variable with the smallest |Z-score| is removed. Watch the bar drop out.
Book Quote
"Backward-stepwise selection starts with the full model, and sequentially deletes the predictor that has the least impact on the fit. The candidate for dropping is the variable with the smallest Z-score." — ESL §3.3.2, Hastie, Tibshirani & Friedman

Requires \(n > p\) to start (full model must be fit). Cannot be used when \(p \gg n\). Forward stepwise can start anywhere; backward requires a fitted full model.

Guarantees
✗
Globally Optimal
Greedy — removing the weakest at each step doesn't guarantee the best final subset.
✗
Works when p > n
Cannot fit full model when p > n. Forward stepwise is required in that regime.
~
Unbiased
Conditionally unbiased on the chosen subset; adaptive selection induces bias.
✗
Low Variance
Z-score-based removal is sensitive to collinearity and small sample fluctuations.
✓
Considers full joint fit
Unlike forward, it starts from the joint OLS model, capturing correlated predictor effects.

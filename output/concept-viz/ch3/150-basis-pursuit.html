Same optimization problem, different communities, different names. Both seek a sparse representation of a signal (or response) in terms of a dictionary (or design matrix).

Conceptual Note

In signal processing, a signal y is decomposed into a sparse linear combination of basis elements (atoms) from an overcomplete dictionary D. Basis Pursuit finds the sparsest such decomposition by minimizing the L1 norm of the coefficients, which is exactly what the Lasso does in the regression context. The constraint form \(\|\beta\|_1 \leq t\) and the Lagrangian form \(+\lambda\|\beta\|_1\) are equivalent via the Lagrange multiplier correspondence \(t \leftrightarrow \lambda\).

Book Quote

"In the signal processing literature, the lasso is also known as basis pursuit." — ESL, Section 3.1

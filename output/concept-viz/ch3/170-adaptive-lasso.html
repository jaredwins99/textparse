Adaptive Lasso

What this fixes vs. vanilla Lasso

Benefits

Oracle consistency while staying convex. Standard Lasso is convex but not oracle-consistent (it cannot simultaneously select the right variables and give unbiased estimates). SCAD achieves oracle consistency but is non-convex. Adaptive Lasso gets both: oracle consistency with a convex objective.
Simple idea: penalize each coefficient differently based on an initial estimate. Large OLS coefficients get small weights (lightly penalized, survive). Small OLS coefficients get large weights (heavily penalized, zeroed).

Tradeoffs

Depends on a good initial estimator. If OLS is unreliable (e.g., p > n where OLS does not exist), you need an alternative initial estimate (ridge, marginal regression).
Still biased in finite samples despite asymptotic oracle properties. The adaptive weights reduce but do not eliminate shrinkage bias.
Extra tuning parameter nu (weight exponent) on top of lambda.
"The adaptive lasso yields consistent estimates of the parameters while retaining the attractive convexity property." -- ESL, Section 3.8.5

How adaptive weights change the penalty per coefficient

Standard Lasso penalizes every coefficient with the same weight. Adaptive Lasso sets \(w_j = 1/|\hat\beta_j^{OLS}|^\nu\): large OLS coefficients get tiny weights (barely penalized), small ones get huge weights (heavily penalized). Drag lambda and nu to see which coefficients survive.

Formula reference

\( \min_\beta \left\{ \|y - X\beta\|^2 + \lambda \sum_j w_j |\beta_j| \right\}, \quad w_j = \frac{1}{|\hat\beta_j^{OLS}|^\nu}, \quad \nu > 0 \)

Large OLS coefficient \(\Rightarrow\) small weight \(\Rightarrow\) less penalized (survives). Small OLS coefficient \(\Rightarrow\) large weight \(\Rightarrow\) heavily penalized (zeroed). This is the mechanism that gives oracle consistency while keeping the objective convex.

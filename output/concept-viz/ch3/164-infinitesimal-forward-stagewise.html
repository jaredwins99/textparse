Infinitesimal Forward Stagewise (FS0)

Concept 164 ESL 3.8 Estimation / Optimization = Lasso path

The ε → 0 limit of incremental forward stagewise: discrete steps become a continuous flow, and the resulting path is identical to the lasso solution path.

Benefits & Tradeoffs
Why does this theoretical limit matter?
Benefits
  • Produces exactly the lasso path -- without an L1 penalty. "As ε → 0, the coefficient profiles... approximate the lasso" (ESL 3.8.1, p. 87). A greedy, iterative procedure recovers the same regularized solution as convex optimization.
  • Gives a boosting interpretation of the lasso. The lasso is not just a penalized regression -- it is also the continuous limit of a forward stagewise boosting procedure. This connects two seemingly different worlds.
  • Smooth, monotone coefficient paths. In the continuous limit, coefficients trace smooth curves. No jagged staircases, no oscillations. Each coefficient enters and grows monotonically.
  • Theoretical bridge. FS0 connects forward stagewise, LAR, and the lasso into a single algorithmic family. Understanding FS0 unifies three sections of the chapter.
Tradeoffs
  • Not directly computable. You cannot actually take infinitely many infinitesimal steps. FS0 is a mathematical object, not a runnable algorithm. To compute the path, use LAR or coordinate descent instead.
  • Equivalence breaks at non-generic configurations. When two predictors are perfectly correlated or the lasso solution is non-unique, FS0 and the lasso paths can differ. "The two paths are typically identical except in cases of non-uniqueness" (ESL 3.8.1).
  • Biased estimates. Like the lasso, FS0 shrinks coefficients toward zero. The bias is the price of sparsity and stability.
  • Continuous-time formulation is conceptually harder. The ODE description requires comfort with differential equations, which adds a barrier to understanding.
"Letting ε → 0 gives the differential equation... We call this limiting procedure infinitesimal forward stagewise regression or FS0." -- ESL 3.8, p. 87

Statistical Guarantees
Equivalent to lasso path YES*
Under general position (no exact ties in correlations), the FS0 path and the lasso solution path are identical. *Can differ when the lasso solution is non-unique.
Monotone coefficient paths YES
Each coefficient evolves monotonically. Once a predictor enters with a positive sign, it stays positive and grows (or stops). No oscillations.
Sparse intermediate solutions YES
At any point along the continuous path, only a subset of coefficients are active. Predictors enter the model one at a time (or in groups when correlations are tied).
Continuous-time ODE form YES
The path satisfies a well-defined differential equation, making it amenable to mathematical analysis and connecting it to the theory of gradient flows.
Prediction consistency YES
Since the path equals the lasso path, all consistency results for the lasso (prediction error bounds, oracle properties under conditions) carry over.
Unbiased NO
Like the lasso, FS0 produces biased estimates -- coefficients are shrunk toward zero. This is by design: the bias buys variance reduction and sparsity.
Formulas -- Reference
$$\dot\beta_j(t) = \mathrm{sign}\bigl(\langle \mathbf{x}_j,\, \mathbf{r}(t)\rangle\bigr) \cdot \mathbf{1}\Bigl[j = \arg\max_k \bigl|\langle \mathbf{x}_k,\, \mathbf{r}(t)\rangle\bigr|\Bigr]$$

The ODE governing FS0: at each instant, only the coefficient of the predictor most correlated with the residual changes, and it changes at unit rate in the direction of the correlation. All other coefficients are frozen.

$$\mathbf{r}(t) = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{X}\boldsymbol\beta(t)$$

The residual vector at arc-length parameter t. As t increases, ||r(t)|| decreases monotonically.

ESL 3.8, p. 87. The parameter t plays the role of arc length (total L1 variation of the coefficient path). It is the continuous analog of the iteration counter in FSε. The path is parametrized inversely to the lasso penalty λ: as t → ∞, the solution approaches OLS (like λ → 0).

Orthogonal Projection

The OLS fitted values ŷ are the orthogonal projection of y onto the column space of X.

What This Means
  • ■ X defines a subspace — the column space col(X) is a plane (or higher-dimensional flat) sitting inside the full data space Rn.
  • ● y lives outside this plane — the observed response vector generally cannot be expressed as a linear combination of the columns of X.
  • ● ŷ is the closest point in the plane to y — "closest" in the Euclidean sense, minimizing RSS = ||y − Xβ||².
  • ⎯ The residual (y − ŷ) is perpendicular to the plane — it points straight "up" from the plane, at a right angle to every column of X.
  • ★ Geometric view makes OLS properties intuitive — idempotency, the normal equations, and why more predictors always reduce RSS all follow immediately from the geometry.

The Right Angle

Why Closest?
Every other point on the plane is farther from y

Key Properties of the Hat Matrix H = X(XᵀX)⁻¹Xᵀ
Definition
ŷ = Hy
H "puts the hat on y" — maps the response to its projection
Idempotent
H² = H
Projecting twice gives the same result as projecting once
Symmetric
Hᵀ = H
H is a self-adjoint (orthogonal) projector
Trace = rank
trace(H) = p + 1
Equals the number of parameters fit (including intercept)
Formulas
\( \hat{\mathbf{y}} = \mathbf{X}\hat{\boldsymbol{\beta}} = \mathbf{X}(\mathbf{X}^\top\mathbf{X})^{-1}\mathbf{X}^\top\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{H}\mathbf{y} \)
\( \mathbf{y} - \hat{\mathbf{y}} \perp \operatorname{col}(\mathbf{X}) \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad \mathbf{X}^\top(\mathbf{y} - \mathbf{X}\hat{\boldsymbol{\beta}}) = \mathbf{0} \)
"We minimize RSS(β) = ||y − Xβ||² by choosing β̂ so that the residual vector y − ŷ is orthogonal to this subspace."

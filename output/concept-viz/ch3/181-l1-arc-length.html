The L1 arc length is a scale-free complexity measure for solution paths. It equals the total amount of "movement" in all coefficients measured by L1 norm. LAR paths have L1 arc length proportional to the number of steps taken.

The L1 arc length (total variation) measures how much the coefficient vector moves along the solution path. For the lasso/LAR path, TV(β, S) = S_max (where S_max is the L1 arc length at the OLS solution) because coefficients move monotonically. This makes the arc length a natural x-axis for plotting coefficient profiles.

Formula

\[\mathrm{TV}(\hat\beta, S) = \int_0^S \|\dot{\hat\beta}(s)\|_1\, ds = \sum_j \mathrm{TV}(\hat\beta_j, S)\]

For a piecewise linear path, TV reduces to \(\sum_j \sum_k |\Delta\hat\beta_j^{(k)}|\) — the total absolute change per predictor. Monotone paths have TV = total L1 norm increase.

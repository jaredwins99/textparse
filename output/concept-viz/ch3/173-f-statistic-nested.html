The F-distribution under H₀. Red shaded region is the critical region (α = 0.05). The blue line shows the observed F value. When signal is zero, F should be near 1. As signal grows, F increases toward rejection.
Book Quotes
"The F statistic measures the change in residual sum-of-squares per additional parameter in the bigger model, and it is normalized by an estimate of σ²." — ESL §3.2.1
"Under the null hypothesis that the smaller model is correct, the F statistic will have a F(p₁−p₀, N−p₁−1) distribution." — ESL §3.2.1
Statistical Properties
YES F-distributed under normality + H₀
YES Handles simultaneous tests
COND Requires models to be nested
COND Robust asymptotically (large N)
NO Not robust to non-normality (small N)
Note: z²-test for single coefficient = F-test with p₁−p₀ = 1. The F-test generalizes to joint hypotheses.

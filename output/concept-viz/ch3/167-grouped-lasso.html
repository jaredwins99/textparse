Grouped Lasso

What this fixes vs. vanilla Lasso

Benefits

Groups of related variables enter or leave the model together. Standard Lasso selects individual variables, which is wrong when predictors come in natural groups (e.g., dummy variables for a categorical factor, polynomial terms for one feature).
The sqrt(p_l) factor corrects for group size, ensuring fair competition between groups of different dimensions.
Still convex -- the L2 norm within each group is convex, so the penalty is convex and the global solution is unique.

Tradeoffs

No within-group sparsity. Either the entire group is in or the entire group is out. Individual variables inside an active group are all retained (unless combined with an L1 penalty, i.e. "sparse group lasso").
Requires knowing the group structure a priori. If groups are misspecified, selection can be worse than individual Lasso.
"This procedure encourages sparsity at both the group and individual levels." -- ESL, Section 3.8.4

Formula reference

\( \min_\beta \left\{ \tfrac{1}{2}\|y - \beta_0 \mathbf{1} - \sum_\ell X_\ell \beta_\ell\|^2 + \lambda \sum_\ell \sqrt{p_\ell}\,\|\beta_\ell\|_2 \right\} \)

\(p_\ell\) = size of group \(\ell\). The \(\sqrt{p_\ell}\) factor normalizes so larger groups don't dominate. \(\|\beta_\ell\|_2\) is the L2 norm within each group -- this is what forces the "all-in or all-out" behavior (compare to L1 norm in standard Lasso which acts on individual coefficients).
